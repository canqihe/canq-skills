# Workflow Examples

Complete examples of interactive movie concept development workflows.

## Example 1: Sci-Fi Thriller

### Initial Request

**User:**
> I want to make a movie about a retired astronaut who discovers Earth is being invaded by aliens, but only his grandchildren believe him.

**Claude:**
Great concept! A few questions to develop this:
- What genre appeals to you? (Sci-Fi thriller, Family adventure, Horror, Drama)
- Who's your target audience? (General, Teenagers, Adults)
- What runtime are you aiming for? (90-150 minutes)
- Any specific tone preferences? (Serious, humorous, dark, hopeful)

### User Clarification

**User:**
- Genre: Sci-Fi thriller
- Audience: Adults
- Runtime: 120 minutes
- Tone: Dark and gritty, like Arrival meets Signs

### Script Development Phase

**Claude (ScriptWriter):**
# Script Outline: The Last Watch

## Logline
A disgraced former astronaut must protect his grandchildren from a silent invasion that only they can see, forcing him to complete the mission that destroyed his career.

## Characters

### Commander Jack "Harrier" Morrison - 62
**Role:** Protagonist
**Personality:** Gritty, disciplined, cynical, fiercely loyal
**Motivation:** Redemption for a failed Mars mission that ended his career
**Sample Dialogue:** "Space doesn't forgive. Neither does the truth."

### Emma Morrison - 12
**Role:** Co-protagonist, Jack's granddaughter
**Personality:** Observant, brave, sees what adults miss
**Motivation:** Believes her grandfather when no one else will
**Sample Dialogue:** "They're not here to hurt us, Grandpa. They're here to replace us."

### Leo Morrison - 15
**Role:** Supporting, Jack's grandson
**Personality:** Skeptical, protective of sister, tech-savvy
**Motivation:** Protect his sister and grandfather
**Sample Dialogue:** "I'm checking the sensors. There's nothing there. But something's there."

### Dr. Sarah Chen - 45
**Role:** Antagonist (initially), former colleague
**Personality:** Ambitious, pragmatic, secretly conflicted
**Motivation:** Career advancement,掩盖真相
**Sample Dialogue:** "Jack, you destroyed your career chasing ghosts. Don't destroy your family too."

### The Visitors (collective entity)
**Role:** True antagonist
**Nature:** Infiltration species, replaces humans silently

## Act Structure

### Act I: The Setup (30 minutes)

**Inciting Incident:**
Jack's grandchildren visit for the weekend. Emma notices their neighbor "Mr. Henderson" acting strangely—moving unnaturally, repeating phrases. Jack dismisses it.

**Character Introductions:**
- Jack: Introduced in his cluttered study, surrounded by space memorabilia, drinking alone
- Emma and Leo: Arrive for weekend visit, strained relationship with grandfather
- Dr. Chen: Contacted by Emma about strange phenomena, dismissive but intrigued

**Status Quo:**
Jack is a broken man, living in isolation, haunted by a failed Mars mission where he claimed to see something that wasn't there.

**First Plot Point:**
Emma shows Jack video footage of "Mr. Henderson" walking through a locked door. Jack recognizes the movement pattern—identical to what he saw on Mars. The invasion has begun.

### Act II: The Confrontation (60 minutes)

**Rising Action:**
- Jack and grandchildren investigate, discover more "replaced" people in town
- Dr. Chen arrives to "help," secretly monitoring Jack
- They realize the scale: 30% of town already replaced
- Jack's old NASA contacts refuse to help, think he's having a breakdown

**Midpoint:**
Jack discovers the Visitors' plan: they don't want to destroy humanity—they want to become it. Complete infiltration over 5 years, starting with small towns.

**Plot Twist 1:**
Dr. Chen reveals she's been replaced weeks ago. The chase begins.

**Plot Twist 2:**
Emma reveals she can communicate with the Visitors—she's developing immunity from proximity to Jack, who was exposed on Mars

**All Is Lost Moment:**
Trapped in Jack's study, surrounded by replaced neighbors and authorities. Jack's old NASA commander (also replaced) demands their surrender over the radio.

### Act III: The Resolution (30 minutes)

**Climax:**
Emma's communication ability reveals the Visitors' weakness: they're hive-mind connected. Jack realizes he can use his old Mars transmission equipment to broadcast a "virus"—Emma's voice, amplified, disrupting their network.

**Resolution:**
The broadcast works. Replaced people collapse, Visitors' consciousness expelled. But not all—some escaped to other towns.

**Character Arc Completion:**
Jack is vindicated but knows this isn't over. He's no longer the broken man—this is his new mission. Emma and Leo are now his crew.

## Key Scenes

1. **Opening Scene:** Jack's nightmare—Mars mission flashback, seeing something in the dust storm (5 minutes)

2. **The Discovery:** Emma shows Jack the door footage, his skepticism shatters (10 minutes)

3. **First Encounter:** Jack confronts "Henderson," realizes it's not human (8 minutes)

4. **Dr. Chen's Arrival:** She seems to help, but subtle hints something's wrong (7 minutes)

5. **The Reveal:** Chen's replacement revealed, chase through small town (12 minutes)

6. **Emma's Connection:** She communicates with a Visitor, learns their plan (10 minutes)

7. **The Stand:** Final confrontation at Jack's house (15 minutes)

8. **The Aftermath:** Jack, Emma, Leo watching news reports of similar incidents nationwide (8 minutes)

## Themes
- **Truth vs. Madness:** What we see vs. what others believe
- **Family as Crew:** The people you trust are your crew, blood or not
- **Redemption:** Second chances to complete the mission
- **Infiltration vs. Invasion:** The fear of losing ourselves, not just our lives

## Workflow Best Practices

### 1. Start Broad, Then Refine

**Don't:**
> "I want a movie about a divorced astronaut who's also a chef and fights crime with his robot dog." (Too specific from the start)

**Do:**
> "I want a movie about someone starting over." Then explore directions through dialogue.

### 2. Embrace Iteration

Most good concepts require 3-5 iterations:
- **Pass 1:** Get the basic idea on paper
- **Pass 2:** Refine character motivations
- **Pass 3:** Sharpen the plot structure
- **Pass 4:** Deepen themes and subtext
- **Pass 5:** Polish dialogue and scene descriptions

### 3. Ask Specific Questions

Instead of: "What do you think?"
Ask: "Should the tone be darker or lighter?" "Who's your ideal lead actor?" "What's your budget range?"

### 4. Use Web Search Strategically

For casting, search for:
- "[Actor name] recent movies 2024 2025"
- "[Actor name] upcoming projects"
- "Actors similar to [Actor name] for comparison"

For script research, search for:
- "Similar movies to [concept] box office"
- "[Genre] conventions and tropes"
- "[Setting] filming locations"

### 5. Document User Preferences

Keep track of:
- Genre preferences
- Tone desires (dark, light, serious, humorous)
- Favorite actors/actresses
- Budget considerations
- Format preferences (theatrical vs. streaming)

Reference these in later iterations for consistency.
